Summary

QNu Labs (est. 2016, Bengaluru) is India's most commercially advanced quantum-safe cryptography company, offering Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) hardware and Quantum Random Number Generators (QRNG) to enterprise, government, and defence clients. The company operates at TRL 5 and is a recognised participant in India's National Quantum Mission. It occupies a strategic position in the quantum-safe communications supply chain, with dependencies on single-photon detectors and optical infrastructure.

QNu Labs was founded in 2016 by a team of physicists and engineers with the mission of making quantum-safe security commercially viable for Indian enterprise and government infrastructure. Operating from Bengaluru, the company has since built a product suite spanning hardware security modules (HSMs), quantum key distribution systems, and quantum random number generators deployed in production environments across BFSI and defence.

The company's core platform relies on quantum key distribution protocols, specifically BB84 and decoy-state variants adapted for metropolitan optical networks. Complementing this, QNu's QRNG modules provide certified entropy sources that exceed classical pseudo-random generators in unpredictability, critical for key generation in high-assurance applications. Both product lines are eligible for procurement support under India's National Quantum Mission, which has earmarked funding for domestic quantum-safe communications infrastructure.

As of March 2026, QNu Labs has completed a Series A funding round and is scaling manufacturing to support broader government procurement timelines under the NQM. The company's roadmap includes integration with post-quantum encryption (PQE) standards finalised by NIST, positioning it at the intersection of hardware and software quantum security — a relatively rare position among Indian deep-tech startups.
